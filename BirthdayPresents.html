Birthday Presents

December 9: Music Box

My friend loves listening to music, and I wanted to make that experience a bit more dynamic for her. I decided to make her a box that, when plugged into an audio source, flashed colored light along with the music. To do this, I used a transistor to regulate voltage running across a series of LEDs according to the amplitude of the audio signal; when the music gets louder, the box gets brighter. A basic circuit diagram is provided below. I used a TIP31c transistor in the circuit.

I built a working prototype on a breadboard as a proof-of-concept using a single LED, a TIP31c transistor, and a 3.5mm audio jack breakout board.

The final project was soldered onto a stripboard using four red LEDs and a TIP31c transistor. Instead of a 9V battery, like the prototype used, I attached a 12V wall-adapter to an XT60 connector, which powered the entire circuit. This was my first time soldering, and I had a lot of fun learning!

Below is a video of the final product working. I made the box out of a 2 Liter bottle, and went over the LEDs to give the light a more diffused characteristic. To improve on this project, I would include an op-amp so that the computer's volume does not have to be very high to trigger the LEDs, and a voltage regulator switch to make sure that the LEDs don't receive too much voltage.

January 25: Hometown Chains

One of my friends is from Taiwan, so I made her something that could remind her of home. I made a 3D printed pendant in the shape of Taiwan that she could wear as a necklace or a bracelet.

The outline of the island was taken from a map of Taiwan, which I converted into SketchUp geometry and manipulated to my desired specifications. The final product was 3D printed in the Boston University Tinker Lab.

February 8: Penguin Power Team

My friend and I met at Boston University's first year student outreach program (FYSOP), which is a program for incoming freshmen to do community service in Boston before school starts so they can get familiar with the city and its culture and neighborhoods and meet new people at the same time. One of the cornerstones of FYSOP is all the silly chanting that everyone does. My friend and my favorite chant was the Penguin Power Team chant, which involves singing with your tongue out, hopping on one foot, and spinning around, all at the same time. Needless to say, for her birthday, I designed and 3D printed a cartoon penguin for her!

I printed the penguin in Boston University's Tinker Lab. Have you seen a penguin power team? Look at me, a penguin you will see.
